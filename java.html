Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens if both OPE1/OPE2 and ET are absent?

A: The grade is U, and the student must repeat the course.

Q: What happens if OPE1/OPE2 is absent but ET is present and T ≥35?

A: The grade is I_OP, and the student must:

  • Complete OPE alone in the next term.
  • GA, quiz, and ET marks will be carried over, OR
  • Repeat the course.

Q: What happens if OPE1/OPE2 is absent, ET is present, and T <35?

A: The grade is U, and the student must repeat the course.

Q: What happens if OPE1/OPE2 is present but the score is <30/100, ET is present, and T ≥40?

A: The grade is I_OP, and the student must:

  • Redo OPE alone in the next term.
  • GA, quiz, and ET marks will be carried over, OR
  • Repeat the course.

Q: What happens if both OPE1/OPE2 and ET are present, but T <40?

A: The grade is U, and the student must repeat the course.

Q: What happens if OPE1/OPE2 is present with a score <30/100, and ET is absent?

A: The grade is I_BOTH, and the student must:

  • Redo the End Term exam and OPE alone in the next term.
  • GA and quiz marks will be carried over, OR
  • Repeat the course.

Q: What happens if OPE1/OPE2 is present with a score >30/100, but ET is absent?

A: The grade is I, and the student must:

  • Complete ET alone in the next term.
  • GA, quiz, and OPPE marks will be carried over, OR
  • Repeat the course.

Q: What happens if both OPE1/OPE2 and ET are present?

A: The grade is based on the total score T.
